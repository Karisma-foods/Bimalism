Biology Questions and Answers: Chapters 1-5

Chapter 1: The Cell

  1. Q1: What is the basic unit of life?
    A: Cell
  2. Q2: Name the organelle responsible for energy production.
    A: Mitochondria
  3. Q3: What is the cell membrane made of?
    A: Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins
  4. Q4: Define osmosis.
    A: Movement of water across a semi-permeable membrane from low to high solute concentration
  5. Q5: Function of ribosomes?
    A: Protein synthesis
  6. Q6: Difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells?
    A: Prokaryotic cells have no nucleus or membrane-bound organelles; eukaryotic cells do.
  7. Q7: Which organelle contains DNA?
    A: Nucleus
  8. Q8: Role of lysosomes?
    A: Digest cellular waste and foreign substances
  9. Q9: What is cytoplasm?
    A: Gel-like substance in the cell where organelles are suspended
  10. Q10: What is the function of the Golgi apparatus?
    A: Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins
  11. Q11: Define homeostasis.
    A: Maintaining internal stability in an organism
  12. Q12: Function of chloroplasts?
    A: Photosynthesis in plant cells
  13. Q13: What is the nucleolus?
    A: Region inside nucleus where ribosomes are made
  14. Q14: Difference between plant and animal cells?
    A: Plant cells have cell walls and chloroplasts; animal cells do not
  15. Q15: What is the main structural support in plant cells?
    A: Cell wall
  16. Q16: Function of vacuoles?
    A: Storage of water, nutrients, and waste
  17. Q17: What is plasmolysis?
    A: Shrinking of cytoplasm due to water loss in plant cells
  18. Q18: What is the role of microtubules?
    A: Support cell shape and help in intracellular transport
  19. Q19: Difference between passive and active transport?
    A: Passive transport requires no energy; active transport requires energy
  20. Q20: What is the fluid mosaic model?
    A: Model describing cell membrane as a dynamic structure with proteins embedded in a lipid bilayer

Chapter 2: Biomolecules

  1. Q1: Name the four major biomolecules.
    A: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Nucleic acids
  2. Q2: Monomer of carbohydrates?
    A: Monosaccharides
  3. Q3: Function of proteins?
    A: Structural support, enzymes, hormones
  4. Q4: Lipids are primarily made of?
    A: Glycerol and fatty acids
  5. Q5: Which biomolecule stores genetic information?
    A: Nucleic acids (DNA and RNA)
  6. Q6: Example of a polysaccharide?
    A: Starch, Glycogen, Cellulose
  7. Q7: Function of enzymes?
    A: Speed up biochemical reactions
  8. Q8: Saturated vs unsaturated fats?
    A: Saturated: single bonds, solid; Unsaturated: double bonds, liquid
  9. Q9: What is a peptide bond?
    A: Bond linking amino acids
  10. Q10: What is RNA made of?
    A: Nucleotides (A, U, G, C)
  11. Q11: Difference between DNA and RNA?
    A: DNA: double-stranded, deoxyribose; RNA: single-stranded, ribose
  12. Q12: Function of glycogen?
    A: Energy storage in animals
  13. Q13: Function of cellulose?
    A: Structural support in plant cell walls
  14. Q14: Name a structural protein.
    A: Collagen, Keratin
  15. Q15: Function of lipids?
    A: Energy storage, insulation, cell membrane component
  16. Q16: What are nucleotides made of?
    A: Sugar, phosphate, nitrogenous base
  17. Q17: Difference between DNA and ATP?
    A: DNA stores genetic info; ATP stores energy for cellular processes
  18. Q18: What is denaturation of proteins?
    A: Loss of protein structure due to heat, pH, or chemicals
  19. Q19: Example of a disaccharide?
    A: Sucrose, Lactose, Maltose
  20. Q20: Importance of biomolecules?
    A: Essential for structure, energy, and information storage in cells

Chapter 3: Cell Cycle and Cell Division

  1. Q1: Phases of cell cycle?
    A: G1, S, G2, M
  2. Q2: Function of interphase?
    A: Cell grows and DNA replicates
  3. Q3: Difference between mitosis and meiosis?
    A: Mitosis produces 2 identical cells; meiosis produces 4 gametes with genetic variation
  4. Q4: Function of spindle fibers?
    A: Separate chromosomes during cell division
  5. Q5: Define cytokinesis.
    A: Division of cytoplasm to form two cells
  6. Q6: What is chromatin?
    A: DNA and protein in non-dividing cell
  7. Q7: What are sister chromatids?
    A: Identical copies of a chromosome joined at centromere
  8. Q8: Significance of meiosis?
    A: Reduces chromosome number and increases genetic variation
  9. Q9: Stages of mitosis?
    A: Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase
  10. Q10: What is metaphase?
    A: Chromosomes align at the cell equator
  11. Q11: Function of centrosome?
    A: Organizes microtubules during cell division
  12. Q12: What is crossing over?
    A: Exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes during meiosis
  13. Q13: Define karyotype.
    A: Number and appearance of chromosomes in a cell
  14. Q14: Function of checkpoints in cell cycle?
    A: Ensure proper cell division
  15. Q15: What is anaphase?
    A: Sister chromatids separate to opposite poles
  16. Q16: What is telophase?
    A: Chromosomes decondense and nuclear envelope reforms
  17. Q17: Difference between somatic and gametic cells?
    A: Somatic: body cells; Gametic: reproductive cells
  18. Q18: Importance of cell division?
    A: Growth, repair, reproduction
  19. Q19: What is a mutation?
    A: Change in DNA sequence
  20. Q20: What is apoptosis?
    A: Programmed cell death
