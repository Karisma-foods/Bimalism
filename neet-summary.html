Physics Summary - Key Concepts for NEET Exam

1. Physical World and Measurement

Physics is the study of nature and its laws, encompassing fundamental forces like gravitational, electromagnetic, strong nuclear, and weak nuclear forces.

Key Concepts

  • Scope and excitement of physics
  • Relationship between physics, technology, and society
  • Fundamental forces: gravitational, electromagnetic, strong, weak
  • Nature of physical laws and their universality

Units and Measurements

The International System of Units (SI) includes seven base units: meter (length), kilogram (mass), second (time), ampere (current), kelvin (temperature), mole (amount of substance), and candela (luminous intensity).

Dimensional Formula: Expresses a physical quantity in terms of fundamental dimensions (M, L, T).

Example: Force = [M L T⁻²]

2. Kinematics

Kinematics studies motion without considering its causes, focusing on parameters like displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Motion in a Straight Line

Describes motion along a linear path with constant or varying acceleration.

v = u + at

s = ut + ½at²

v² = u² + 2as

Motion in a Plane

Projectile motion involves motion under constant gravitational acceleration with initial velocity components.

Time of flight: T = 2u sinθ / g

Maximum height: H = u² sin²θ / 2g

Horizontal range: R = u² sin2θ / g

3. Laws of Motion

Newton's laws are the cornerstone of classical mechanics, governing the behavior of objects under the influence of external forces.

Newton's Laws

  • First Law: An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force (Inertia).
  • Second Law: F = ma (Force = mass × acceleration).
  • Third Law: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

Friction

Friction opposes relative motion between contacting surfaces, categorized as static or kinetic.

Static friction: f_s ≤ μ_s N

Kinetic friction: f_k = μ_k N

4. Work, Energy and Power

Work is the transfer of energy when a force causes displacement. Energy exists as kinetic or potential, and power measures the rate of work done.

Work: W = F ⋅ s = F s cosθ

Kinetic energy: K = ½mv²

Potential energy: U = mgh

Power: P = W/t = F ⋅ v

Conservation Laws

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed between forms (kinetic, potential, etc.).

5. Motion of System of Particles and Rigid Body

Studies the motion of multiple particles or rigid bodies, focusing on center of mass and rotational dynamics.

Key Concepts

  • Center of mass and its motion
  • Moment of inertia and torque
  • Angular momentum and its conservation
  • Rotational kinematics and dynamics

Torque: τ = r × F

Angular momentum: L = Iω

Rotational kinetic energy: K = ½Iω²

6. Gravitation

Gravitation governs the attractive force between masses, described by Newton's universal law of gravitation.

Gravitational force: F = GMm/r²

Gravitational potential energy: U = -GMm/r

Escape velocity: v_e = √(2GM/r)

Kepler's Laws

  • First Law: Planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.
  • Second Law: Equal areas are swept in equal times.
  • Third Law: T² ∝ r³ (Period squared proportional to radius cubed).

7. Properties of Bulk Matter

Explores mechanical properties of solids and fluids, including elasticity, viscosity, and surface tension.

Mechanical Properties of Solids

Young’s modulus: E = stress/strain

Stress = F/A, Strain = ΔL/L

Mechanical Properties of Fluids

Pressure: P = F/A

Buoyant force: F_b = ρVg (Archimedes’ principle)

8. Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics deals with heat, work, and energy transfer in systems.

Laws of Thermodynamics

  • Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium defines temperature.
  • First Law: ΔU = Q - W (Change in internal energy = heat added - work done).
  • Second Law: Entropy of an isolated system always increases.

9. Behavior of Perfect Gas and Kinetic Theory

Describes the behavior of ideal gases and the kinetic theory of gases.

Ideal gas law: PV = nRT

Kinetic energy of gas: KE = 3/2 kT (per molecule)

10. Oscillations and Waves

Studies periodic motion (oscillations) and wave propagation.

Simple harmonic motion: F = -kx

Wave speed: v = fλ

Time period: T = 2π√(m/k) for a spring
