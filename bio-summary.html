Class 11 Biology Syllabus

Unit 1: Diversity in Living World

  • Understanding biodiversity and classification systems.
  • Studying taxonomy, binomial nomenclature, and five-kingdom classification.
  • Emphasizing plant and animal kingdom diversity.

Unit 2: Structural Organisation in Animals and Plants

  • Exploring plant tissues and anatomy (root, stem, leaf).
  • Analyzing animal tissues and morphology (e.g., frog).
  • Focusing on structural adaptations.

Unit 3: Cell Structure and Function

  • Detailing cell theory, prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic cells.
  • Covering cell organelles and their functions.
  • Including cell division (mitosis, meiosis).

Unit 4: Plant Physiology

  • Investigating photosynthesis, respiration, and transpiration.
  • Studying plant growth hormones and photoperiodism.
  • Emphasizing water and mineral nutrition.

Unit 5: Human Physiology

  • Exploring digestion, respiration, and circulation.
  • Covering excretion, neural control, and coordination.
  • Focusing on endocrine system and locomotion.

Class 12 Biology Syllabus

Unit 6: Reproduction

  • Studying asexual and sexual reproduction in plants.
  • Analyzing human reproductive health and reproductive technologies.
  • Emphasizing reproductive hormones.

Unit 7: Genetics and Evolution

  • Detailing Mendelian inheritance and molecular basis of inheritance.
  • Covering DNA replication, transcription, and translation.
  • Exploring evolutionary theories and evidence.

Unit 8: Biology and Human Welfare

  • Investigating human health and common diseases.
  • Studying immunity and vaccines.
  • Covering strategies in improvement in food production.

Unit 9: Biotechnology and Its Applications

  • Exploring principles of biotechnology.
  • Analyzing genetic engineering and its applications.
  • Emphasizing biosafety and ethical issues.

Unit 10: Ecology and Environment

  • Studying ecosystems, biodiversity, and conservation.
  • Covering environmental issues and pollution control.
  • Emphasizing population and community ecology.
